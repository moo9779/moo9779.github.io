Battle of Fort Sumter

  • The Battle of Fort Sumter was the bombardment of Fort Sumter near Charleston, South Carolina from April 12013, 1861. The South Carolina militia, later part of the Confederate Army, attacked Fort Sumter, and symbolically the United States, in order to gain secession from the Union.
  • There were no casualties nor was there a major fight. There were exchange of mortar rounds but no one got hurt. The attack on the fort was a symbolic gesture by the South Carolina militia to secede from the United States. The United States surrendered Fort Sumter and abandoned its facilities in Charleston. With the Battle of Fort Sumter and the secession of South Carolina and other southern states from the Union, it was a catalyst for the American Civil War, the bloodiest war in US history.
