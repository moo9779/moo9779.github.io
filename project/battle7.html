Second Battle of the Marne

  • The Second Battle of the Marne was the last major German offensive on the Western front during the First World War. The German offensive lasted from 15 July – 6 August 1918 on the Marne River, near Paris, France. There was a stalemate due to the tough environment of the Marne river being a natural barrier for the Allied forces. It was easy to dig deep trenches on both sides and difficult to maneuver tanks and soldiers across the river due to the fast currents of the river and the surrounding muddy environments. As a result, the German’s previous offensive, known as the Spring Offensive, was a complete failure.
  • After the Spring Offensive failed, the German high officers sought planned one more offensive to defeat the Allied forces across the Marne. German generals wanted to attack through Belgium and decided to have a large diversion force attack through Marne in order to divert the Allied forces away from Belgium into the Paris part of Marne. As a result, the main German force was near Belgium, not Paris. However, the main Allied forces were stationed near the Paris part of Marne. When the second German offensive began, known as the Second Battle of the Marne, the Allied forces not only stopped the large diversion force, but also because it was not the main German force, the Allied forces were able to launch a large counter-offensive attack and break through the German line. This broke the 3 year stalemate on the Marne River.
  • This battle was one of the bloodiest of the war. For the Allied forces, the French suffered 95,000 casualties, Americans 45,000, British 17,000 and Italian, 9000. The Germans suffered 140,000 casualties and another 30,000 were captured.
  • Because the German line was broken, the Germans lost not only the battle, but also the Great War. The victory was a huge morale booster for the Allied forces and immediately after the Second Battle of Marne they were gaining victory after victory over the German forces, known as Hundred Days Offensive. In three months, the Great War ended.
